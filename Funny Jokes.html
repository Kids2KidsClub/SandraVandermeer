Funny Jokes And Riddles To Tell Your Friends

The number of stars on the side of the riddle tells how hard it is. Good luck!

****Q: You are in a room. Inside the room, there is 3 light switches, a chair, and a door. On the other side of the door, there is 1 lightbulb. Your challenge is to find out which light switch turns on the bulb, but... you're only allowed to enter and exit room 2 once, and you're only allowed to turn on 2 of the switches. And, your not allowed to look inside the room with the light. How do you figure out which switch turns on the light?

A: First, you turn on switch #1 and wait outside of the room for 15 or so minutes. Then, you turn off the switch and turn on switch #2. Proceed to the room. If the light is on, then you know it is switch #2. If not, then touch the light bulb. If it is burning hot, then you know the switch is switch #1. If it isn't hot, then the correct switch will be switch #3.

*Q: A cowboy leaves town on Friday, stays for two days, and leaves on Friday. How can that be?

A: His horse is named Friday.

*Q: A monkey is tied to the end of a piece of string 1 m (3 ft) long. How did it manage to eat some figs from a bowl 3 m (9 ft) away?

A: The other end of the string wasn't tied to anything!

**Rosie's mother has three daughters. She has chosen their names very carefully. The oldest is called April, and the middle one is called May. What is the youngest one called?

A: Rosie!

**Q: Please don't drop me or I will crack. Give me a smile and I will smile back. What am I?

A: A mirror

****Q: What is always hot, even if you keep it in the fridge?

A: Chili pepper!

***Q: A man must cross the river in his rowboat, taking with him a snake, a mouse, and a sack of grain. The boat is only big enough to carry the man and one item at a time. He can't leave the snake and the rat together, and he can't leave the rat and the sack of grain together. How does he get everything safely across the river?

A: First he takes the rat across and rows back. Then, he takes the snake across, bringing the rat back with him. Lastly he takes the sack of grain across and goes to the other side to get the rat, and brings the rat to the other side.

**Q: If a fisherman brings home 20 buckets of fish, and his father brings home 40 bockets, who has the most fish?

A: You have the most fish because your father is only carring buckets and you are carring fish.
